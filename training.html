Training

Intro Clojure Lab

Dates: April 18-19th, 9 am - 5 pm (2 days)
Instructors: Alex Miller (@puredanger), Katherine Fellows (@kf)
Price: $300 (includes lunch both days)

This course is intended for new Clojure developers with prior programming experience. We expect that prior to the class you will have installed a REPL and spent a few hours reading an introductory Clojure text. Some recommended texts include Clojure for the Brave and True, Clojure From the Ground Up, Clojure Programming, Programming Clojure, and Practical Clojure. Sessions will include some lecture but will primarily consist of hands-on guided labs and workshops.

The goal of the course is to achieve a thorough understanding of the basics of Clojure syntax and usage. Topics will include the Clojure syntax and evaluation model, collections, sequences, functions, flow control, namespaces, records, protocols, multimethods, and state.

The course covers two full days (Sat Apr 18 and Sun Apr 19, 9-5 pm) and includes lunch, snacks, and beverages both days (all other meals on your own). Wifi and power will be provided. Attendees are expected to bring a laptop - instructions will be sent later on workshop setup.

About the instructors: Alex Miller works on Clojure and supports the community at Cognitect. He co-authored the forthcoming book, Clojure Applied (Pragmatic Press).

Katherine Fellows serves on the Board of Directors for ClojureBridge and enjoys teaching functional programming through Clojure. Currently, she's a Scala developer by trade, building distributed systems on the Core Application Platforms team at Comcast.

Macros

Dates: April 19th, 9 am - 11 am (2 hrs)
Instructor: Colin Jones (@trptcolin)
Price: $150

In this workshop, which assumes only basic Clojure knowledge (see Clojure Koans or any of the excellent introductory Clojure books), we'll dig into how Clojure macros work, some tricks of the macro-writing trade, and the inherent tradeoffs we must accept when we reach for macros as a tool.

The workshop will include lecture and Q&A time, as well as plenty of hands-on exercises to apply and practice the concepts we talk about. Come with a laptop or a friend to pair with, and you'll leave with a solid grasp of when and how to use macros (and also your laptop or friend!).

About the instructor: Colin Jones is director of software services at 8th Light, where he crafts reliable, maintainable software for clients large and small. He's worked on a number of open-source Clojure projects, including the Clojure Koans, REPLy, Leiningen, and small contributions to Clojure itself. Colin recently published his first book, Mastering Clojure Macros (Pragmatic Press).

core.async

Dates: April 19th, 3 - 5 pm (2 hrs)
Instructor: Luke VanderHart (@levanderhart) Price: $150

The course will cover core.async, a library for Clojure and ClojureScript for concurrent programming with immutable values. Topics covered will include the basics of channels and go blocks, higher level channel operations and constructs, and helpful patterns and practices for systems.

The course will be held Sunday Apr 19, 3-5 pm. Wifi and power will be provided. Attendees are expected to bring a laptop - instructions will be sent later on workshop setup.

About the instructor: Luke VanderHart is a Clojure/ClojureScript developer, writer and speaker. He is the co-author of Practical Clojure (Apress, 2010), ClojureScript: Up and Running (O'Reilly 2012) and the Clojure Cookbook (O'Reilly 2014). Luke lives in Durham, NC and works for Cognitect.
